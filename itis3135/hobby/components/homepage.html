What is Golf?

Golf is a precision sport where players use various clubs to hit a ball into a series of holes on a course in the fewest possible strokes. Unlike many sports, golf doesn’t have a standard playing field—each course has unique landscapes, hazards, and layouts that challenge players differently.

Basic Rules of Golf

  1. The Objective – Get the ball into the hole in as few strokes as possible.
  2. Scoring – The number of strokes per hole determines a player's score (lower is better).
  3. Equipment – Players use different clubs for different shots, from drivers for long distances to putters for short, precise strokes.
  4. Etiquette – Golf has unwritten rules about respect, such as letting faster players play through and avoiding distractions while others are taking a shot.

Why Golf is Unique

  • Unlike most sports, golf is played against the course, not just an opponent.
  • Players of all skill levels can enjoy the game.
  • It’s both a mental and physical challenge.
